Your role matters

Faith leaders & registrars: guardians of fairness

Whether you conduct Nikahs or register marriages, you are uniquely placed to prevent harm and uphold women’s Islamic and legal rights.

Why the UK? Recent reports (Muslim Women’s Network UK, 2022) show that many Muslim women enter marriages unaware of their rights, and unregistered Nikahs can leave them without legal protection. Your guidance can change that.

🕌 For faith leaders

imams · nikah officiants · mosque elders

You are trusted to explain the sacred contract. Use that trust to ensure brides know their rights — and that those rights are written into the Nikah.

  • Educate before the ceremony: Explain that the Qur’an (5:1) and the Prophet ﷺ require conditions to be honoured. Share our plain‑language guides with couples.
  • Encourage written conditions: The Nikah can (and should) include clauses on monogamy, delegated divorce, financial independence, etc. Show them example wordings.
  • Discuss the civil marriage: In the UK, a Nikah alone is not legally recognised unless registered. Advise couples to also have a civil ceremony to ensure protection under English/Welsh/Scottish law.
  • Be alert to pressure: If a bride seems reluctant or pressured, pause and offer private, confidential support. You can refer to forced marriage unit.

Key message: “The believers are bound by their conditions” – you are implementing the Sunnah by helping women secure fair terms.

📋 For marriage registrars

registration officers · local authority · celebrants

You ensure marriages are legally sound. Many Muslim couples believe their Nikah is enough – you can help them understand the law and avoid future vulnerability.

  • Explain the two-step process: A Nikah (religious ceremony) does not create a civil marriage in UK law. Couples must also have a civil ceremony at a register office or approved venue.
  • Spot potential red flags: If one party seems uninformed, or if there are signs of coercion, you have a duty to pause and, if needed, contact the Forced Marriage Unit (020 7008 0151).
  • Provide information: Keep copies of our information sheet for couples explaining why civil registration matters for inheritance, divorce, and housing rights.
  • Work with faith leaders: Build relationships with local imams to encourage joint counselling and proper registration.

Statutory guidance: The Marriage Act 1949 (as amended) and the Marriage (Same Sex Couples) Act 2013 set out your duties; the Forced Marriage Unit offers free training.

⚖️ UK legal landscape at a glance

🏴 England & Wales

Only a civil ceremony (or a religious ceremony that also complies with civil law) gives legal validity. A Nikah conducted in a registered building can be legally binding if all civil requirements are met. Otherwise, a separate civil marriage is essential.

🏴 Scotland

Marriage law differs: religious and belief celebrants can be temporarily authorised. Still, a Nikah must be registered with the district registrar. Couples should check their celebrant is approved.

🇮🇪 Northern Ireland

Similar to England: religious marriages can be legally recognised only if conducted by a registered celebrant in a registered building. Many Nikahs are not legally recognised – civil registration is vital.

🔍 Forced Marriage Unit (FMU): 020 7008 0151 / 020 7008 1500 · 24‑hour support. If you suspect coercion, call before the ceremony.

🗂️ Quick reference

For faith leaders – three key actions:

  1. Discuss rights & conditions pre‑Nikah.
  2. Ensure the Nikahnama includes agreed clauses.
  3. Advise civil registration & signpost to legal help.

For registrars – three key actions:

  1. Explain legal status of Nikah vs civil marriage.
  2. Watch for signs of pressure/coercion.
  3. Provide information sheet (keep copies handy).
📢 Training & workshops: Muslim Women’s Network UK and the Forced Marriage Unit offer free online training for professionals. Request a session for your mosque or registration service.

🚨 If someone is in immediate danger, call 999. For forced marriage concerns, contact the Forced Marriage Unit on 020 7008 0151 (office hours) or 020 7008 1500 (out of hours).
